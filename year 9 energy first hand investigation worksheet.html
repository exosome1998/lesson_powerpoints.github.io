Part D: Experimental Design

9. Identify the variables.

Type of light globe (Incandescent vs LED).
Dependent (Measure):
Surface Temperature (°C).
10. Controlled Variables (Fair Test):
List TWO things kept constant.
1. Starting room temperature.
2. Time the lights were left on.
11. Reliability vs Validity:
Why repeat the test 3 times?
To improve Reliability. It helps identify outliers (mistakes)
and provides a more accurate average.

Part E: Advanced Efficiency

Formula: Efficiency (%) = (Useful ÷ Total) × 100
12. Calculate Efficiency: CFL Globe
Input: 15J. Useful Light: 4J.
(4 ÷ 15) × 100 = 26.6% Efficient.
13. Calculate Efficiency: LED Globe
Input: 10J. Useful Light: 8.5J.
(8.5 ÷ 10) × 100 = 85% Efficient.
14. Comparison:
How many times more efficient is the LED (85%) than Incandescent (5%)?
85 ÷ 5 = 17 times more efficient.

Part F: Critical Thinking

15. The "Rebound Effect":
LEDs are cheap to run, so people leave them on longer. Explain the problem.
If used longer, total energy use stays high,
cancelling out savings and creating CO2 pollution.
16. Safety & Disposal:
CFLs contain Mercury. Why is this a problem?
Mercury is toxic. If broken or thrown in landfill,
it poisons the environment.
17. Future Tech: Bioluminescence
Scientists are making lights from bacteria. Give one pro and one con.
Pro: No electricity needed (Natural).
Con: Might not be bright enough for reading.

Conclusion

18. Summarise the relationship between Power (Watts) and Heat.
Direct link: Globes that require High Power (Watts) for the same brightness
waste that extra energy as Heat.
Low Power (LED) = Low Heat.
