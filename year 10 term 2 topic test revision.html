Topic 15: Mini Explanation Practice

139. Explain why noble gases are usually unreactive.
They have full outer electron shells, so they are stable.
140. Explain why ionic compounds have no overall charge.
The positive and negative charges balance.
141. Explain why a carbonate fizzes in acid.
Carbon dioxide gas is produced.
142. Explain why a metal fizzes in acid.
Hydrogen gas is produced.
143. Explain why pH can increase during a carbonate-acid reaction.
The acid is being used up, so the solution becomes less acidic.
144. Explain why catalysts are useful.
They make reactions faster and are not used up.
145. Explain why heating a reaction often makes it faster.
Particles move faster and collide more often with enough energy.
146. Explain why higher concentration makes a reaction faster.
There are more particles per volume, so collisions happen more often.
147. Explain why a precipitate forms.
Two ions join to form an insoluble solid.
148. Explain what a spectator ion is.
An ion that stays dissolved and does not take part in the main reaction.
