1a. Decoding Scientific Words

Complex biology words are just small puzzle pieces glued together. If you know the code, you can read anything!

🔍 The Prefixes (The Start of the Word)

♨️ THERMO = Heat
☀️ PHOTO = Light
🧪 CHEMO = Chemical
🤏 MECHANO = Touch / Physical
⚡ NOCI = Pain / Damage
🔊 AUDIO = Sound
💧 OSMO = Water / Solutes
🎈 BARO = Air/Blood Pressure

🧩 The Suffix (The Ending)

Receptor = A Receiver / Detector.

It is the specialized cell or organ in the body that "receives" or detects a specific change in the environment.

Example:

Noci + receptor = A Pain Detector!

2a. Writing Scientific Sequences

Scientists use precise "connective transition words" to explain cause and effect, turning a visual flowchart into a professional paragraph.

🗺️ Reading the Stimulus-Response Pathway

🔗 The "Connective" Words

Instead of drawing arrows, we use these phrases to link the steps:

  • First, the stimulus happens...
  • which is detected by the receptor...
  • sending a signal to the control centre...
  • which then triggers the effector...
  • resulting in a response.

❌ Bad Example

"The fire was hot so I moved my hand. My brain told my muscles to do it because it hurt."

✅ Good Scientific Example

First, the hot fire (stimulus) occurs, which is detected by thermoreceptors in the skin. This sends a signal to the spinal cord, which then triggers the arm muscle (effector), resulting in pulling your hand away.

3a. Diagram Literacy: Reading the Reflex Arc

Science isn't just text. You must know how to translate an anatomical diagram into a sequence.

Tracing the Pathway

In a reflex diagram, follow the arrows. They represent the electrical signal traveling through the nerves. A reflex is special because it skips the brain and loops through the spinal cord for maximum speed!
