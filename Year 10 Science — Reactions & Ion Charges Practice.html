4. Quick formula rule
Criss-cross charges

1. Write the positive ion first. 2. Write the negative ion second. 3. Balance total charge to zero.

Mg²⁺ + Cl⁻ → MgCl₂

Al³⁺ + SO₄²⁻ → Al₂(SO₄)₃

5. Precipitation reactions
General pattern

Two clear solutions mix. An insoluble solid forms.

solution + solution → precipitate + solution

AgNO₃(aq) + NaCl(aq) → AgCl(s) + NaNO₃(aq)

Common insoluble precipitatesExample
Most carbonatesCaCO₃(s)
Most hydroxidesCu(OH)₂(s)
Silver halidesAgCl(s)
Barium sulfateBaSO₄(s)
Lead(II) iodidePbI₂(s)
6. Single replacement reactions
General pattern

A more reactive metal replaces a less reactive metal from a compound.

A + BC → AC + B

Zn(s) + CuSO₄(aq) → ZnSO₄(aq) + Cu(s)

Mini reactivity order: Mg > Al > Zn > Fe > Cu > Ag

A reaction happens only if the single metal is higher in the reactivity series.
7. Metal + acid reactions
General pattern

metal + acid → salt + hydrogen

Mg + 2HCl → MgCl₂ + H₂

AcidSalt endingExample salt
Hydrochloric acidchlorideMgCl₂
Sulfuric acidsulfateZnSO₄
Nitric acidnitrateCa(NO₃)₂
8. Metal carbonate + acid
General pattern

metal carbonate + acid → salt + water + carbon dioxide

CaCO₃ + 2HCl → CaCl₂ + H₂O + CO₂

Gas test: Carbon dioxide turns limewater milky/cloudy.

9. Word equation checklist

Identify the reaction type, choose the correct products, then balance the symbol equation.

Example: zinc + hydrochloric acid → zinc chloride + hydrogen

Zn + 2HCl → ZnCl₂ + H₂

I. Challenge: explain your thinking

1. Why does MgCl₂ need two chloride ions?

Mg is Mg²⁺ and each chloride is Cl⁻, so two chloride ions balance one magnesium ion.

2. A student writes CaCO₃ + HCl → CaCl + H₂O + CO₂. Find two mistakes.

Calcium chloride should be CaCl₂, and the equation needs 2HCl to balance.

3. What gas is produced when a metal reacts with an acid?

Hydrogen gas, H₂.
