What is a Force?

✏️ A Force is a PUSH, PULL, or TWIST.
Forces make things move or change shape. We measure Force in Newtons (N).

Newton's 1st Law: Inertia

✏️ Objects keep doing exactly what they are already doing.
They stay still, or keep moving straight, UNLESS a force pushes or pulls them.
Basically: Objects are LAZY! (Inertia)

Inertia in Real Life: The Skateboard

The skateboard hits a rock and stops instantly.
Your body's inertia wants to KEEP moving forward at the exact same speed!

Inertia in Space: The Moon

The Moon wants to fly off in a straight line (Inertia).
Earth's gravity pulls it back, creating a curved orbit!

Newton's 2nd Law: F = m × a

✏️ Force = Mass × Acceleration
To make a heavy object move fast, you need a HUGE push.

F = ma: Changing the Mass

We apply the EXACT same Force (50 N).
The light box accelerates fast. The heavy box barely moves!

F = ma: Changing the Force

We push the EXACT same car.
More force = Faster acceleration!

Calculation: Finding Force

"A 5 kg bowling ball accelerates at 3 m/s². Find the Force (F)."

1: Formula → F = m × a
2: Numbers → F = 5 × 3
3: Answer → F = 15 N

Calculation: Finding Acceleration

"You push a 20 kg cart with a force of 100 N. Find Acceleration (a)."

1: Formula → a = F ÷ m
2: Numbers → a = 100 ÷ 20
3: Answer → a = 5 m/s²

Calculation: Finding Mass

"A dog pulls a sled with 200 N. It accelerates at 4 m/s². Find Mass (m)."

1: Formula → m = F ÷ a
2: Numbers → m = 200 ÷ 4
3: Answer → m = 50 kg

Newton's 3rd Law: Action & Reaction

✏️ Every Action has an Equal and Opposite Reaction.
Forces ALWAYS come in pairs. You cannot touch something without it touching you back!

3rd Law Example: Rockets!

There is no air in space to push against!
The rocket pushes fuel DOWN. The fuel pushes the rocket UP!

3rd Law Example: The Balloon

Letting go of an untied balloon makes it zip away!
The balloon pushes air LEFT. The air pushes the balloon RIGHT.
