Module 1: Kinetic Energy

Formula: KE = 0.5 √ó Mass √ó (Velocity)¬≤

1. Scenario: The Heavy Truck vs. Fast Car

Which has more energy? Calculate both to check.

Truck: Mass 2000kg @ 10m/s.

Calc: 0.5 √ó 2000 √ó 100 (10¬≤) = 100,000 J.

Sports Car: Mass 1000kg @ 30m/s.

Calc: 0.5 √ó 1000 √ó 900 (30¬≤) = 450,000 J.

Conclusion: Which factor increases energy more?

Answer: Speed / Velocity has a bigger effect than mass because it is Squared (¬≤).

Module 2: GPE (Heights)

Formula: GPE = Mass √ó Gravity √ó Height (g=10)

1. Scenario: The Bungee Jump

A 60kg jumper stands on a ledge 50m high.

a) Calculate their GPE at the top:

Calc: 60 √ó 10 √ó 50 = 30,000 J.

b) They jump! Halfway down (25m), what happens to their GPE?

Answer: It has Halved / Decreased.

c) Where did that missing GPE go?

Answer: It converted into Kinetic Energy (Movement).

2. Spot the Mistake:

Tom lifts a 10kg weight. He says he used 20J of energy. He lifted it 2 meters high.

Check his math: 10kg √ó 10(g) √ó 2m = 200 J.

Tom is wrong by a factor of 10 (he likely forgot Gravity).

Module 3: Efficiency & Waste

Formula: (Useful Energy √∑ Total Input) √ó 100

1. Scenario: Gaming PC vs Laptop

A Gaming PC draws 500W from the wall. Only 300W is used for processing the game. The rest is heat.

a) How much energy is wasted as heat?

Calc: 500 - 300 = 200 Watts.

b) Calculate the efficiency of the PC:

Calc: (300 √∑ 500) √ó 100 = 60 %.

2. Visualising Waste (Sankey Diagram):

Draw an arrow splitting into two. The straight arrow is USEFUL. The arrow bending down is WASTED.

Data: Input 100J -> Useful 20J -> Waste 80J.

(Draw Sankey Diagram)

Which arrow should be thicker? The Waste Arrow (80J).

Module 4: Boss Battle (Conservation)

Physics Law

"Energy cannot be created or destroyed." This means in a perfect system (no friction), the GPE lost falling = the KE gained moving.

1. The Pendulum Swing:

A pendulum has 50J of GPE at its highest point.

a) What is its Kinetic Energy (KE) at the very bottom?

Answer: 50 J (All GPE turns to KE).

b) What is its KE at the highest point (where it stops for a split second)?

Answer: 0 J (It is not moving).

2. The Rollercoaster Challenge (Reverse Calc):

We know a rollercoaster car has 40,000J of GPE.

The Mass of the car is 800kg.

How high is the drop? (Recall: GPE = m √ó g √ó h)

Step 1: 40,000 = 800 √ó 10 √ó h

Step 2: 40,000 = 8000 √ó h

Step 3: 40,000 √∑ 8000 = 5 meters.
