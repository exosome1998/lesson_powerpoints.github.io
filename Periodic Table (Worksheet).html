L1 — What is the Periodic Table?★☆☆

1. The periodic table shows all known chemical elements.

2. Each element has a name and a chemical symbol.

3. Elements are arranged by increasing atomic number.

4. (a) Element / (b) Compound — oxygen is shown on the table. (circle)

5. The symbol for iron is Fe.

6. True or False: The periodic table helps scientists classify elements. True

L2 — Metals: Where are they?★☆☆

1. Most elements on the periodic table are metals.

2. Metals are mainly found on the left and in the middle.

3. Metals are usually shiny, strong and good conductors.

4. (a) Sodium / (b) Oxygen is a metal. (circle)

5. The staircase line separates many metals from non-metals.

6. Iron, copper and aluminium are examples of metals.

L3 — Non-metals: Where are they?★★☆

1. Most non-metals are found on the right side of the periodic table.

2. Hydrogen is a non-metal found on the far left.

3. Non-metals are usually poor conductors of heat and electricity.

4. (a) Chlorine / (b) Iron is a non-metal. (circle)

5. Many non-metals are gases, such as nitrogen and oxygen.

6. Solid non-metals are often dull and brittle.

L4 — Numeracy: Atomic Number📊

1. The atomic number tells us how many protons are in an atom.

2. Hydrogen has atomic number 1.

3. Carbon has atomic number 6.

4. Oxygen has atomic number 8.

5. Elements are ordered by increasing atomic number.

6. (a) Protons / (b) Neutrons — atomic number counts these. (circle)

L5 — Groups: Columns★★☆

1. A vertical column on the periodic table is called a group.

2. Elements in the same group often have similar properties.

3. Group 1 elements are called the alkali metals.

4. Group 7 elements are called the halogens.

5. Group 0 or 18 elements are called noble gases.

6. (a) Down / (b) Across — groups go ______. (circle)

L6 — Periods: Rows★★☆

1. A horizontal row on the periodic table is called a period.

2. Periods go across from left to right.

3. The first period contains hydrogen and helium.

4. The second period starts with lithium and ends with neon.

5. (a) Row / (b) Column — a period is a... (circle)

6. Elements change gradually across a period.

L7 — The Staircase Line★★☆

1. The staircase line helps separate metals and non-metals.

2. Elements near the staircase are often called metalloids.

3. Metalloids can have properties of both metals and non-metals.

4. Silicon is a useful metalloid.

5. (a) Silicon / (b) Sodium is near the staircase. (circle)

6. Silicon is used in computer chips.

L8 — Master: Locate an Element★★★

1. To locate an element, use its group and period.

2. The group tells the vertical column.

3. The period tells the horizontal row.

4. Carbon is in period 2.

5. Sodium is in group 1.

6. Explain how to find chlorine: Use its group and period

L9 — Group 1: Alkali Metals★★☆

1. Group 1 elements are called alkali metals.

2. Lithium, sodium and potassium are in group 1.

3. They react with water to make hydrogen gas.

4. (a) Potassium / (b) Neon is an alkali metal. (circle)

5. Alkali metals are stored under oil because they are reactive.

6. Reactivity increases as you go down group 1.

L10 — Group 2 Metals★★☆

1. Group 2 elements are called alkaline earth metals.

2. Magnesium and calcium are in group 2.

3. Group 2 metals are reactive, but usually less reactive than group 1.

4. Calcium is important for strong bones and teeth.

5. (a) Magnesium / (b) Chlorine is a group 2 metal. (circle)

6. Magnesium burns with a bright white flame.

L11 — Group 7: Halogens★★★

1. Group 7 elements are called the halogens.

2. Fluorine, chlorine, bromine and iodine are halogens.

3. Halogens are non-metals.

4. They react with metals to form salts.

5. (a) Chlorine / (b) Sodium is a halogen. (circle)

6. Reactivity decreases as you go down group 7.

L12 — Numeracy: Symbols & Groups📊

1. Sodium, Na, is in group 1.

2. Magnesium, Mg, is in group 2.

3. Chlorine, Cl, is in group 7.

4. Neon, Ne, is in group 0/18.

5. How many elements are shown above? 4

6. (a) Na / (b) Cl is an alkali metal. (circle)

L13 — Group 0/18: Noble Gases★★☆

1. Noble gases are found in group 0/18.

2. Helium, neon and argon are noble gases.

3. Noble gases are very unreactive.

4. They are found on the far right of the periodic table.

5. (a) Neon / (b) Lithium is a noble gas. (circle)

6. Helium is used in balloons because it is very light.

L14 — Key Vocabulary★☆☆

1. Group: a vertical column.

2. Period: a horizontal row.

3. Metal: usually a good conductor and often shiny.

4. Non-metal: usually a poor conductor.

5. Metalloid: has properties of metals and non-metals.

6. Symbol: (a) one or two letters / (b) a full sentence. (circle)
