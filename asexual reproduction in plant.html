Plant Cloning: Asexual Reproduction

Unit: Biology | Year 8
Introduction: Most plants reproduce using seeds (sexual reproduction), but some have a "shortcut" called Asexual Reproduction. A single parent plant uses its own cells to grow a new offspring. Because there is no mixing of DNA, the new plant is a clone (genetically identical) of the parent. This is often called Vegetative Propagation.

1. Stolons (Runners)

Stolons are specialized stems that grow horizontally above the ground. As they stretch out, they form "nodes". When a node touches the soil, it grows adventitious roots down and leaves up, creating a self-sufficient clone.

Ex: Strawberry plants carpet a garden using runners.

2. Rhizomes

Rhizomes are stems that grow horizontally underground. They are thick and fleshy because they store energy (starch). New upward shoots push through the soil surface from the rhizome's nodes to form new plants.

Ex: Ginger, Bamboo, and Turmeric.

3. Suckers

Suckers (or root sprouts) emerge directly from the roots of a parent plant. If a tree is damaged, chopped down, or highly stressed, it activates dormant buds on its roots to send up new shoots nearby.

Ex: Cherry trees and Blackberry bushes.

4. Bulbs

A Bulb is an underground structure with a tiny central stem wrapped in thick, fleshy storage leaves (layers). Small "offset" baby bulbs grow attached to the base of the parent and can be separated.

Ex: Onions, Tulips, Garlic, and Daffodils.

5. Tubers

Tubers are swollen, underground stems stuffed with starch. They feature small bumps called "eyes", which are actually dormant buds. Each single eye can sprout into a completely new plant.

Ex: Potatoes (Stem tuber) and Yams.

6. Plantlets

Plantlets are unique miniature clones that develop directly on the edges of the parent plant's leaves. They grow their own tiny roots while still attached, eventually dropping off to take root instantly.

Ex: Kalanchoe (Mother of Thousands) and Spider Plant.

The Big Picture: Advantages, Disadvantages & Artificial Propagation

Pros & Cons of Cloning: Asexual reproduction is incredibly efficient. A plant does not need to waste energy making nectar, colorful petals, or waiting for bees to pollinate it. It can rapidly take over a patch of ideal soil. However, the major disadvantage is a lack of genetic variety. Because every plant is a clone, a single disease or pest that can kill one plant will likely wipe out the entire population.

Artificial Propagation (Human Intervention): Humans have learned to hijack these natural cloning abilities for farming. We use Cuttings (snipping off a piece of stem and placing it in water until it grows roots) and Grafting (taping a branch from an excellent fruit tree onto the roots of a strong tree). Without artificial cloning, the apples, grapes, and bananas you buy at the store wouldn't taste the same every time!

Part A: Key Terminology
1. Define Asexual Reproduction in your own words.
Reproduction requiring only one parent, producing genetically identical offspring (clones) without seeds.
2. Why are the offspring of asexual reproduction referred to as "clones"?
Because they contain the exact same DNA / genetic code as the single parent plant.
3. What is the physical difference between a Stolon and a Rhizome?
A stolon grows horizontally ABOVE the ground. A rhizome grows horizontally UNDER the ground.

[ T / F ] True (Using cuttings and grafting)

Part E: Scientific Analysis
13. Look at the diagram of the Bulb. Why do you think the bulb is made of thick, fleshy layers? What is their function?
The layers act as a food storage unit. They store energy (starch) so the plant can survive dormant periods like cold winters.
14. Scenario: A farmer plants a massive field of potatoes using tubers cut from a single parent plant. A new fungus disease arrives in the wind.
What will likely happen to the rest of the field? Explain why.
The entire field will likely be wiped out. Because all the potatoes are clones, they share the exact same genetic weaknesses. If the disease kills one, it can kill them all.
15. If you wanted a plant to rapidly cover a large, bare patch of soil in your garden, would you choose a plant that uses Bulbs or a plant that uses Stolons? Explain your reasoning based on the text.
Stolons. Stolons are specialized for rapid horizontal spreading above ground. Bulbs mostly stay in one place, slowly producing small offsets right next to the parent.
16. What are the "eyes" on a potato tuber? If you cut a potato in half, making sure both halves have at least one eye, and plant them, what will happen?
The eyes are dormant buds. Both halves will sprout their own stems and grow into two separate, identical potato plants.
Part F: Artificial Propagation
17. Why do humans use Grafting for fruit trees instead of planting seeds from the fruit?
Seeds create random genetic mixes (might taste bad). Grafting clones the parent, guaranteeing the fruit tastes exactly the same.
18. Describe the process of taking a "Cutting."
Snipping a piece of stem from a parent plant and placing it in water/soil until it grows new roots.
