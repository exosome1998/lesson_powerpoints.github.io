A. Literacy: The Vacuum Problem

Fill in the blanks using the word bank to apply the concept.

Bank: [ Particles, Waves, Vacuum, Infrared ]

Conduction and Convection both require Particles to move heat. However, Space is a Vacuum (it is empty).

Heat from the Sun reaches Earth by travelling as Waves. This type of invisible light is called Infrared Radiation.

Thinking: The Thermos Flask

A thermos has a vacuum gap (no air) between the inner and outer wall.

Question Reasoning
1. Can conduction happen across the gap? No, because there are no solids touching.
2. Can convection happen across the gap? No, because there is no air/gas to move.
3. Can radiation cross the gap? Yes, waves can travel through empty space.

B. Real Life: Surfaces & Survival

Different surfaces behave differently with radiation. Apply the rule: Black Absorbs/Emits, Silver Reflects.

1. The Car Bonnet Scenario:

Two cars are parked in the blazing sun. One is Matte Black, one is Shiny Silver.

Which car bonnet will fry an egg faster?

Answer: The Black car, because it is a good Absorber of radiation.

2. The Marathon Runner:

Runners are wrapped in shiny "Foil Blankets" after a race to stop them getting hypothermia (too cold).

Why isn't the blanket painted black?

Answer: A black blanket would Emit (give out) their body heat. The foil blanket Reflects the heat back to their body.
