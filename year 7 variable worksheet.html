A. Definitions (Recall)

Use the word bank to complete the definitions.

Bank: [ Measure, Same, Change, Independent, Dependent ]

1. The Independent Variable is the variable that I Change at the start of the experiment.

2. The Dependent Variable is the variable that I Measure at the end (the result).

3. The Controlled Variables are the things I keep the Same to make it a fair test.

B. Literacy: Spot the Variable

Read the stories. Identify the Independent (Change) and Dependent (Measure) variables.

Story 1 (Easy)

"A student changes the temperature of the water to see how fast sugar dissolves."

  • Independent (Change): Water Temperature
  • Dependent (Measure): Speed of dissolving

Story 2 (Medium)

"Sarah wants to see if adding fertilizer makes plants grow taller. She gives Plant A 0ml, Plant B 5ml, and Plant C 10ml. After 2 weeks, she measures the height."

  • Independent: Amount of Fertilizer
  • Dependent: Height of Plant
  • Controlled: Type of Plant / Soil

Story 3 (Hard)

"An engineer tests three different brands of phone battery. He puts them into the same phone playing the same video. He records the time until the phone dies."

  • Independent: Brand of Battery
  • Dependent: Time lasted
  • Controlled: The Phone / Video

C. Fair Testing

Scenario 1: The Race

Story: Ben wants to see who runs fastest. He wears running shoes. He makes Tom wear heavy boots. They run 100m. Ben wins.

1. Is this a fair test? No.

2. Why? Because the Footwear / Shoes was not kept the same.

Scenario 2: Fix the Experiment

Story: Jenny wants to test if hot water cleans dishes faster than cold water. She uses hot water with lots of soap, and cold water with no soap.

3. Jenny made a mistake! She changed two things:

  1. The Temperature (Good)
  2. The Amount of Soap (Bad)

4. To make it fair, she must use the Same amount of soap in both bowls.

D. From Question to Variables

Look at the scientific questions below. Circle or identify the variables.

Q1: "Does the angle of a ramp affect how far a car rolls?"

  • IV (Change): Angle of ramp
  • DV (Measure): Distance car rolls

Q2: "Does the size of a parachute affect how slowly it falls?"

  • IV (Change): Size of parachute
  • DV (Measure): Speed of fall

Q3: "How does the amount of sleep affect student test scores?"

  • IV (Change): Amount of Sleep
  • DV (Measure): Test Score

E. True or False?

1. In a fair test, you change everything. False
2. The Dependent variable is the result. True
3. Controlled variables must stay the same. True
4. You can have two Independent variables. False
5. Graphs usually show the IV on the bottom. True
