L1 — Key Groups★☆☆

1. Vertical columns on the Periodic Table are called Groups.

2. Elements in the same group have similar Chemical properties.

3. Metals are found on the (a) Left / (b) Right side of the table.

4. Non-metals are found on the Right side of the table.

5. Group 18 elements are unreactive and called Noble Gases.

L2 — Reading Element Cards🔍

1. The top number is the Atomic Number. It tells us the number of Protons (and electrons in a neutral atom).

2. Sodium (Na) has an Atomic Number of 11.

3. Sodium's electron configuration is 2, 8, 1.

4. Oxygen (O) has 8 total electrons.

5. Oxygen's electron configuration is 2, 6.

L3 — Electrons & Valency★★☆

1. Electrons sit in energy levels called Shells.

2. The outermost shell is known as the Valence shell.

3. Atoms are most stable when their outer shell is Full.

4. The 'Rule of 8' for a full outer shell is the Octet rule.

5. An element's Group Number tells us its number of Valence electrons.

L4 — Forming Ions (Metals vs Non-metals)★★☆

1. When an atom gains or loses electrons, it becomes an Ion.

2. Metals (Groups 1, 2, 13) want to Lose electrons to empty their outer shell.

3. Losing negative electrons makes metals Positive (+) ions (cations).

4. Non-metals (Groups 15, 16, 17) want to Gain electrons to reach 8.

5. Gaining negative electrons makes non-metals Negative (-) ions (anions).

L5 — Configurations & Ions🔄

1. A Sodium atom (2,8,1) loses 1 valence electron. Its new configuration is 2,8.

2. A Chlorine atom (2,8,7) gains 1 electron. Its new configuration is 2,8,8.

3. Both ions now have the same stable configuration as a Noble Gas.

4. Magnesium (2,8,2) will lose 2 e⁻ to become Mg²⁺.

L6 — Ion Quick Fire⚡

Determine the charge these elements will form to become stable:

1. Potassium (K), Group 1: +1

2. Calcium (Ca), Group 2: +2

3. Aluminum (Al), Group 13: +3

4. Phosphorus (P), Group 15: -3

5. Sulfur (S), Group 16: -2

6. Fluorine (F), Group 17: -1

7. Argon (Ar), Group 18: 0 (Stable)

L7 — Ionic Bonding Rules★★☆

1. Ionic bonds occur between a metal and a Non-metal.

2. Opposites attract! Positive and negative ions are held by Electrostatic forces.

3. In an ionic compound, the total overall charge must equal Zero.

4. (a) Shared / (b) Transferred — Electrons in ionic bonds are ____. (circle)

L8 — Master Challenge 1: The Basics★★★

1. If Barium is in Group 2, its valency (charge) is +2.

2. If Nitrogen is in Group 15, it has 5 valence electrons.

3. To reach a full 8, Nitrogen must (a) gain 3 / (b) lose 5 electrons.

4. The charge of a Nitrogen ion (Nitride) is -3.

5. Group 18 Noble gases do not form ions because their shells are Full.

L9 — The Criss-Cross Method🔄

1. Write the symbol and charge of the Metal first.

2. "Criss-cross" the numerical value of the charges to become Subscripts.

3. Drop the + and - signs when you (a) cross them / (b) keep them.

4. Do you write a subscript of 1? (a) Yes / (b) No

5. If the charges are exactly the same (e.g., +2 and -2), they Cancel out.

L10 — Writing Formulas Practice★★☆

Use the criss-cross method to write the final formula:

1. Na⁺ and F⁻ → NaF

2. Mg²⁺ and Br⁻ → MgBr₂

3. K⁺ and O²⁻ → K₂O

4. Al³⁺ and Cl⁻ → AlCl₃

5. Ca²⁺ and O²⁻ → CaO (Remember to cancel/simplify!)

6. Li⁺ and N³⁻ → Li₃N

7. Be²⁺ and P³⁻ → Be₃P₂

L11 — Naming Rules★☆☆

1. In an ionic name, the Metal always goes first.

2. Oxygen changes its name to Oxide.

3. Nitrogen changes its name to Nitride.

4. Phosphorus changes its name to Phosphide.

L12 — Ionic Lattices & Properties💎

1. Ionic compounds form continuous 3D grids called Crystal Lattices.

2. Because the bonds are very strong, they have High melting points.

3. They conduct electricity ONLY when Melted / Dissolved in water.

L15 — Transition Metals & Roman Numerals🛡️

1. Transition metals (Groups 3-12) can have Variable / Multiple charges.

2. We use a Roman numeral to tell us the Charge of the metal.

3. Copper(I) chloride uses Cu⁺. The formula is CuCl.

4. Copper(II) chloride uses Cu²⁺. The formula is CuCl₂.

5. What is the charge of Iron in Iron(III) oxide? +3
