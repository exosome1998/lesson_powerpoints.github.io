A ray hits the vertical mirror at 40° to the normal. Complete the path as it hits the second mirror.

Ans: Ray reflects at 40°, then hits floor mirror at 50° incidence, reflecting at 50°.

3. Multiple Choice Quick-Fire

1. The angle of incidence is strictly measured between the incident ray and the:
A) Mirror surface B) Normal line C) Reflected ray D) Focal point
Ans: B
2. An image formed by a standard flat (plane) mirror is always:
A) Real & inverted B) Real & upright C) Virtual & inverted D) Virtual & upright
Ans: D
3. Which type of reflection occurs when light hits a rough surface like paper?
A) Specular B) Diffuse C) Converging D) Total Internal
Ans: B

4. Short Answer Concepts

State the primary Law of Reflection mathematically:
Ans: i = r (Angle of incidence = Angle of reflection)
What happens to the angle of reflection if a ray hits a mirror perfectly perpendicular (90°) to the surface?
Ans: 0° (It bounces straight back along the normal).
Explain "lateral inversion" in a plane mirror.
Ans: Left and right appear reversed in the reflection.
A fixed light ray hits a mirror. If the mirror is rotated 15° clockwise, how many degrees does the reflected ray rotate?
Ans: 30° (It rotates twice the angle of the mirror).

If a material has a very high refractive index, what does this tell you about the speed of light inside it?
Ans: Light travels very slowly.
Rank the following from lowest to highest optical density: Water, Diamond, Air, Glass.

If a light ray travels straight through a glass block without bending at all, what must its angle of incidence be?
Ans: 0° (It is traveling directly along the normal).

3. Application Short Answers

The "Invisible" Rod: If a glass pyrex rod is placed in cooking oil with the exact same refractive index as the glass, what happens to light passing through?
Ans: No refraction occurs; the rod seemingly disappears/becomes invisible.
Why do swimming pools look shallower than they actually are?
Ans: Refraction bends light away from the normal as it leaves water, tricking the brain into seeing a higher image.
Why are diamonds cut with so many precise, angled facets?
Ans: To trap light via Total Internal Reflection, forcing it to bounce around before exiting out the top (sparkle).
A ray hits a mirror at 30° to the normal. If the mirror is moved 5cm backward (staying parallel), does the angle of reflection change?
Ans: No. Reflection angle depends strictly on surface orientation, not distance.
Why is the word "AMBULANCE" printed backward on the front of emergency vehicles?
Ans: Due to lateral inversion, drivers looking in their rear-view mirrors will read the word correctly.
