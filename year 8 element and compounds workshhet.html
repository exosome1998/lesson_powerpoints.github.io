L1 — What is an Element?★☆☆

1. An element is a substance made from only one type of atom.

2. Oxygen, gold and iron are examples of elements.

3. Elements are shown on the periodic table.

4. (a) Element / (b) Mixture — copper is one type of atom. (circle)

5. The symbol for oxygen is O.

6. True or False: Elements can be broken down into simpler substances by normal chemical reactions. False

L2 — Metals ⚙️★☆☆

1. Metals are usually shiny and solid at room temperature.

2. Metals conduct heat and electricity well.

3. Metals can be bent or hammered into shape. This is called malleable.

4. (a) Copper / (b) Oxygen is a metal. (circle)

5. Iron is used to make strong steel.

6. Aluminium is useful because it is light and does not rust easily.

L3 — Non-metals 🌬️★★☆

1. Non-metals are usually poor conductors of heat and electricity.

2. Many non-metals are gases, such as oxygen and nitrogen.

3. Solid non-metals are often dull and brittle.

4. (a) Sulfur / (b) Iron is a non-metal. (circle)

5. Carbon can exist as diamond or graphite.

6. Oxygen is needed for animals to breathe/respire.

L4 — Numeracy: Symbols & Names📊

1. The symbol H stands for hydrogen.

2. The symbol O stands for oxygen.

3. The symbol C stands for carbon.

4. Sodium has the symbol Na.

5. How many different element symbols are shown above? 4

6. (a) One / (b) Two letters — Some element symbols have ______ letters. (circle)

L5 — What is a Compound?★★☆

1. A compound is made when two or more different elements chemically join.

2. Water is a compound made from hydrogen and oxygen.

3. Carbon dioxide is made from carbon and oxygen.

4. (a) Element / (b) Compound — H₂O is a... (circle)

5. Compounds usually have different properties from the elements that make them.

6. Sodium chloride is the chemical name for table salt.

L6 — Water: H₂O 💧★★☆

1. Water has the chemical formula H₂O.

2. It contains two hydrogen atoms and one oxygen atom.

3. Water is useful for drinking, washing and growing plants.

4. (a) Compound / (b) Element — Water is a... (circle)

5. Water is usually a liquid at room temperature.

6. Pure water is colourless and has no smell/odour.

L7 — Carbon Dioxide: CO₂★★☆

1. Carbon dioxide has the formula CO₂.

2. It contains one carbon atom and two oxygen atoms.

3. Carbon dioxide is a gas at room temperature.

4. Plants use carbon dioxide during photosynthesis.

5. (a) Yes / (b) No — Is CO₂ a compound? (circle)

6. CO₂ is also found in fizzy drinks.

L8 — Master: Elements vs Compounds★★★

1. An element has only one type of atom.

2. A compound has two or more different elements chemically joined.

3. Oxygen gas, O₂, is an element because both atoms are oxygen.

4. Water, H₂O, is a compound because it has hydrogen and oxygen.

5. (a) O₂ / (b) CO₂ is an element. (circle)

6. Explain why NaCl is a compound: It contains sodium and chlorine

L12 — Formula Counting📊

1. H₂O has 2 hydrogen atoms.

2. H₂O has 1 oxygen atom.

3. CO₂ has 2 oxygen atoms.

4. O₂ is an element because it only contains oxygen.

5. NaCl contains 2 different elements.

6. (a) H₂O / (b) O₂ has two different types of atom. (circle)

L13 — Key Vocabulary★☆☆

1. Atom: a tiny particle that makes up matter.

2. Element: a substance made of one type of atom.

3. Compound: two or more different elements chemically joined.

4. Property: a feature or characteristic of a material.

5. Conduct: to allow heat or electricity to pass through.

6. Brittle: (a) breaks easily / (b) bends easily. (circle)

5. (a) Metal / (b) Plastic would be better for the handle. (circle)

6. Explain your choice for the handle: Plastic is a poor conductor of heat
