Part B: Insect Pollination

Case Study 1: The Golden Wattle

The Golden Wattle (Acacia pycnantha) is Australia's floral emblem. It has bright yellow, fluffy flowers arranged in small balls. It produces a sweet scent and a moderate amount of sticky pollen. It attracts many small insects, particularly beetles and native bees, which crawl over the flower balls.

VERB: DESCRIBE = Say what it is (Features)
Sentence Starter: "The Golden Wattle features..." or "It looks like..."

1. Describe the features of the Golden Wattle that attract insects.

The Golden Wattle has Bright / Yellow flowers that are easily seen. It produces a Sweet Scent to signal food is available.

VERB: EXPLAIN = Say HOW or WHY it works
Sentence Starter: "The pollen is sticky because..." or "This allows..."

2. Explain why the pollen needs to be sticky.

The pollen is sticky so that it Attaches / Sticks to the body of the bee or beetle. This ensures the insect carries it to the next Flower / Stigma.

Part C: Mammal Pollination

Case Study 2: The Banksia & The Possum

Many Australian Banksias are pollinated by mammals, like the Honey Possum or Sugar Glider. These animals are much heavier than bees! To support them, Banksia flowers grow on a tough, woody spike. They produce huge amounts of nectar (energy) and often smell musky or "mouse-like" rather than sweet. They are often dull in color because mammals usually visit at night and use smell, not sight.

1. Compare the features:

Feature Wattle (Insect) Banksia (Mammal)
Smell Sweet Musky / Mouse-like
Strength Delicate Strong / Woody
Nectar Small amount Large amount
VERB: EXPLAIN = Cause and Effect

2. Explain why the Banksia must be strong and woody.

Because mammals are heavy.

Part D: Evaluation Task

VERB: EVALUATE = Make a judgment & support it
How to Evaluate: 1. Make a choice (Judgment).
2. Give a reason "For" (Pro).
3. Give a reason "Against" (Con).
4. Conclusion.
Scenario: The Windy Cliff

Imagine you are planting a garden on a very windy cliff-top in Australia. There are very few insects because the wind blows them away. However, there are many birds and small possums living in the bushes.

Question: Evaluate which plant would survive best on the cliff: The Wattle (Insect pollinated) or the Banksia (Bird/Mammal pollinated)?

Step 1: Make your Judgment.

I think the Banksia would be the most successful plant.

Step 2: Provide Evidence (The "Why").

Use the word "because" to connect your idea.

It would succeed because the local pollinators are Possums / Birds, not insects. Also, insects cannot fly well in the Wind.

Step 3: Consider the Alternative (The "Counter").

The Wattle would fail because...

The wind would blow the insects away.

Part F: Bird Pollination (Extension)

The Kangaroo Paw

The Red-and-Green Kangaroo Paw has a long, tubular shape. Insects cannot reach the nectar at the bottom! Only birds with long beaks (like Honeyeaters) can drink the nectar. As the bird pushes its head in, the anthers brush pollen onto the bird's head.

1. Describe the shape of the flower.

It is long and Tubular.

2. Explain why this shape stops insects.

Insects are too small and don't have long Beaks / Tongues to reach the nectar.

3. Draw a line of logic:

Bird drinks nectar -> Pollen rubs on Head -> Bird flies to next flower -> Pollen rubs on Stigma.
