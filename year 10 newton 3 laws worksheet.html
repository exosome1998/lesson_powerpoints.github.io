L1: What is a Force? /6

1. A force is simply a Push, pull, or twist.

2. Forces can make objects speed up, slow down, or change Direction.

3. We measure force in standard units called Newtons.

4. The symbol used for this unit is ( N / F ).

5. Forces are vectors, meaning they have a size and a Direction.

6. Gravity is a constant ( Push / Pull ) force downwards.

L2: Contact vs Non-Contact /6

1. Kicking a ball is a Contact force.

2. Magnetism is a Non-Contact force.

3. Friction only happens when surfaces ( Touch / Separate ).

4. Gravity pulls you down without touching you. It is Non-Contact.

5. Air resistance pushing against a car is a ( Contact / Non-Contact ) force.

6. Static electricity repelling hair is Non-Contact.

L3: Net Force (Balanced) /6

1. "Net Force" means the Total overall force.

2. If 10N pulls left and 10N pulls right, the Net Force is 0 N.

3. When Net Force is zero, the forces are Balanced.

4. A balanced object will ( Accelerate / Stay Still ).

5. If it was already moving, it keeps moving at a Constant speed.

6. Balanced forces ( Do / Do Not ) change an object's motion.

L4: Newton's 1st Law /6

1. The 1st Law is often called the Law of Inertia.

2. Objects are "lazy" and want to keep doing Exactly what they are doing.

3. An object at rest will stay at Rest.

4. An object in motion stays in motion unless acted on by an ( Unbalanced / Balanced ) force.

5. When a skateboard hits a rock, the rider flies ( Forward / Backward ).

6. The heavier an object is, the ( More / Less ) inertia it has.

L5: Inertia in Space /6

1. The Moon's inertia makes it want to fly in a Straight line.

2. Earth's Gravity pulls it inwards.

3. Forward speed plus inward pull creates a curved Orbit.

4. If Earth vanished, the Moon would fly away in a ( Curve / Straight line ).

5. Satellites use this same principle to circle the Earth.

6. In space, objects ( Do / Do not ) need engines to keep moving at a steady speed.

L6: Newton's 2nd Law /6

1. The 2nd Law connects Force, Mass, and Acceleration.

2. The formula is written as F = m × a.

3. To find Acceleration, the equation is a = F ( ÷ / × ) m.

4. Mass must be measured in Kilograms (kg).

5. Acceleration is measured in meters per second squared (m/s²).

6. 1 Newton of force moves 1 kg at a rate of ( 1 / 10 ) m/s².

L7: F=ma (Changing Mass) /6

1. If you push a light box and heavy box with the Same force...

2. The light 10kg box will have a ( Huge / Tiny ) acceleration.

3. The heavy 1000kg box will have a Tiny acceleration.

4. This proves that more mass requires More force to move.

5. Acceleration is ( Inversely / Directly ) proportional to mass.

6. A fully loaded truck is much ( Harder / Easier ) to stop.

L8: F=ma (Changing Force) /6

1. If we push the exact Same car with two different forces...

2. A small push gives a Small acceleration.

3. A massive push gives a ( Massive / Tiny ) acceleration.

4. To make a car go faster quicker, the engine needs more Force.

5. Force is ( Inversely / Directly ) proportional to acceleration.

6. Doubling the pushing force will Double the acceleration.

L9: F=ma Calculations 1 /6

1. A 90kg man accelerates at 3 m/s². His force is 270 N.

2. A 10kg ball is pushed with 50N. Acceleration (a = F÷m) is 5 m/s².

3. A dog pulls with 100N, causing 2m/s² accel. Mass (m = F÷a) is 50 kg.

4. A 2000kg car accelerates at 4 m/s². The engine force is 8000 N.

5. A 500N force pushes a 100kg cart. It accelerates at 5 m/s².

6. If force is 0N, acceleration must be 0 m/s².

L10: Mass vs Weight (W=mg) /6

1. Mass is the amount of stuff in you, measured in Kilograms.

2. Weight is a FORCE caused by gravity, measured in Newtons.

3. The formula is Weight = Mass × Gravity.

4. On Earth, gravity accelerates things at roughly 9.8 m/s².

5. If you go to the Moon, your ( Weight / Mass ) changes.

6. If you go to the Moon, your ( Weight / Mass ) stays exactly the same.

L11: Newton's 3rd Law /6

1. For every action, there is an Equal and opposite reaction.

2. Forces always come in Pairs.

3. A rocket works by pushing exhaust gas ( Up / Down ).

4. In response, the gas pushes the rocket Up.

5. You ( Can / Cannot ) have a single isolated force in the universe.

6. If you punch a wall with 100N, the wall hits your hand with 100N.

L12: 3rd Law Examples /6

1. An untied balloon shoots to the Right because air shoots left.

2. When swimming, you push water backwards to go Forward.

3. A bird pushes air DOWN to fly Up.

4. Stepping off a small boat makes the boat move Backwards.

5. The force on the boat is exactly ( Equal / Half ) to the force on you.

6. A gun recoils backward because the bullet shoots Forward.
