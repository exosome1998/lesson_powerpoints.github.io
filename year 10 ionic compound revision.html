⚙️ Transition Metals

Transition metals can form ions with different charges. We use Roman Numerals in the name to tell us the exact charge of the metal.

MetalRoman NumeralCharge
Iron (II)(II)Fe²⁺
Iron (III)(III)Fe³⁺
Copper (I)(I)Cu⁺
Copper (II)(II)Cu²⁺
Lead (IV)(IV)Pb⁴⁺
⚠️ Exceptions: Silver is always Ag⁺ and Zinc is always Zn²⁺. They do not need Roman Numerals.
✍️ The Criss-Cross Method

Use this step-by-step method to turn a name into a chemical formula.

Example 1: Magnesium Chloride

Step 1: Write Symbols & Charges

Magnesium is Group 2: Mg²⁺

Chlorine is Group 7: Cl⁻

Step 2: Criss-Cross the numbers to the bottom

The 2 goes to the Cl. The 1 goes to the Mg.

Mg1Cl2

Step 3: Simplify (Drop 1s, divide if possible)

MgCl2

Example 2: Lead(IV) Oxide

Step 1: Write Symbols & Charges

Lead has a (IV), so its charge is: Pb⁴⁺

Oxygen is Group 6: O²⁻

Step 2: Criss-Cross

Pb2O4

Step 3: Simplify

Both 2 and 4 can be divided by 2.

PbO2

Example 3: Aluminum Oxide

Step 1: Write Symbols & Charges

Aluminum is Group 3: Al³⁺

Oxygen is Group 6: O²⁻

Step 2: Criss-Cross

Al2O3

Step 3: Simplify

2 and 3 cannot be divided. The formula stays Al₂O₃.

🗣️ Naming Ionic Compounds

Rule 1: The Metal always goes first.

Rule 2: The non-metal goes second, but change the ending to -ide.

NaCl = Sodium Chloride

Rule 3: If the metal is a Transition Metal, you MUST use a Roman Numeral to show its charge.

⏪ Working Backwards (Un-crossing)

To name a transition metal compound, you must "un-cross" the formula to find the original charge!

Example: Name Fe₂O₃

Step 1: Separate the elements

Fe and O

Step 2: Un-cross the little numbers up to the top

The 3 goes back up to the Fe. The 2 goes to the O.

Fe3+    O2-

Step 3: Check the non-metal

Oxygen is in Group 6, so it SHOULD be 2-. Since this matches, the Fe charge is definitely 3+.

Step 4: Write the name with the Roman Numeral

Iron (III) Oxide

⚠️ The Simplification Trap: Name CuO

Step 1: Un-cross the invisible 1s

Cu₁O₁   →   Cu1+ and O1-

Step 2: Check the non-metal

Wait! Oxygen must ALWAYS be 2-.

Step 3: Multiply to fix

Multiply both charges by 2 to get the real ratio: Cu2+ O2-.

Answer: Copper (II) Oxide

E — Working Backwards

A scientist discovers a new compound with the formula XCl₄.

1. Uncross the numbers to find the charge of Element X:

Cl is -1. There are 4 of them (total -4).
Therefore, X must have a charge of +4.

2. Which Group must Element X belong to?

Group 4 (or a Transition Metal with IV).

Another compound has the formula X₂S₃.

3. What is the exact charge of Element X? Show how you checked.

Uncross: X gets the 3 (+3), S gets the 2 (-2).
Since Sulfur is naturally -2, X must be exactly +3.

A third compound has the formula Z₃N₂.

4. What is the exact charge of Element Z? Which group is it in?

Uncross: Z is +2, N is -3. N matches perfectly.
Z is exactly +2. It must be in Group 2.

A final transition compound is RT₂. (Where T is Oxygen).

5. What is the Roman numeral for R? (Beware the trap!)

Uncross: R1+, O2-? Incorrect, O must be 2-.
Multiply by 2. R is actually +4. Roman numeral is (IV).
F — Spot the Mistake

Identify the mistake in each answer below, and provide the correct formula or name.

1. "Magnesium Oxide = Mg₂O₂"

Mistake: Did not simplify. Correct: MgO

2. "CuCl₂ = Copper Chloride"

Mistake: Missing Roman Numeral. Correct: Copper (II) Chloride

3. "Calcium Phosphide = CaP"

Mistake: Did not criss-cross charges. Correct: Ca₃P₂

4. "Aluminum Oxide = Al₃O₂"

Mistake: Crossed numbers backwards. Correct: Al₂O₃

5. "Ag₂O = Silver (I) Oxide"

Mistake: Silver is an exception (no numeral). Correct: Silver Oxide

6. "NaO = Sodium Oxide"

Mistake: Criss-cross Na(+1) and O(2-). Correct: Na₂O

7. "Iron (III) Oxide = Fe₃O₂"

Mistake: Crossed numbers backwards. Correct: Fe₂O₃

8. "Zinc (II) Chloride = ZnCl₂"

Mistake: Zinc doesn't need a Roman numeral. Correct: Zinc Chloride

9. "CaF₂ = Calcium Fluorine"

Mistake: Forgot to change ending to -ide. Correct: Calcium Fluoride

10. "PbO₂ = Lead (II) Oxide"

Mistake: Did not un-simplify. O is 2-. Correct: Lead (IV) Oxide
