Module 1: Kinetic Energy

The Energy of Movement

"Who rode an E-Scooter or bike recently? Today we calculate how dangerous that is."

The Formula

Kinetic Energy (KE)

KE = 0.5 √ó m √ó v¬≤

  • m = Mass (kg)
  • v = Velocity (m/s)
  • Crucial Step: You must SQUARE the speed first!

Practice Round

The E-Scooter

A teenager on an E-scooter (Total Mass 70kg) travels at 6 m/s.

1. Square Velocity (6¬≤) 6 √ó 6 = 36
2. Setup Formula 0.5 √ó 70 √ó 36
3. Answer 35 √ó 36 = 1,260 J
"Look at Student Ben's answer. He is wrong. Why?"

Spot the Mistake

The Fast Car

Scenario: A 1000kg car moving at 20 m/s.

Student Ben's Working:
KE = 0.5 √ó 1000 √ó 20
KE = 500 √ó 20
KE = 10,000 J
‚ùå The Error:

Ben forgot to SQUARE the velocity!

He used 20. He should have used 400 (20¬≤).

Correct Answer: 0.5 √ó 1000 √ó 400 = 200,000 J

Real Life Science

Why Speed Kills

Why is a crash at 60 km/h so much worse than 30 km/h?

Speed 30 km/h Energy = 1x
Speed 60 km/h (Double) Energy = 4x
Speed 90 km/h (Triple) Energy = 9x

Because of v¬≤, small increases in speed create MASSIVE increases in energy. This is why braking distance gets so long at high speeds.

Module 2: GPE

Gravitational Potential Energy

"This is energy stored in height. Think of lifting weights at the gym."

The Formula

GPE

GPE = m √ó g √ó h

  • m = Mass (kg)
  • g = Gravity (We use 10 N/kg)
  • h = Height (m)
Paste Gym Image

Context: The Gym

The Deadlift

A bodybuilder lifts a 100kg barbell to a height of 2m.

1. Identification m=100, g=10, h=2
2. Calculation 100 √ó 10 √ó 2
3. Answer 2,000 J

If he drops it, that 2,000J turns into sound and impact damage!

"Why does a phone break more often when you are on a call vs texting?"

Scenario Comparison

The Phone Drop

Phone Mass = 0.2 kg. Gravity = 10.

A: Dropped from pocket (1m) 0.2 √ó 10 √ó 1 = 2 J
B: Dropped from ear (1.8m) 0.2 √ó 10 √ó 1.8 = 3.6 J

Dropping from your ear has nearly double the energy. That extra energy is what cracks the screen.

Module 3: Efficiency

Phones, Consoles & Heat Waste

"Why does your phone get hot when you play Roblox/Fortnite? That heat is WASTED energy."

The Concept

Useful vs Wasted

  • Input: Total energy putting in (Battery/Electricity).
  • Useful: What you want it to do (Light/Sound/Processor).
  • Wasted: What you don't want (Heat).
  • Formula: (Useful √∑ Total) √ó 100
Paste Charger Image

Tech Analysis

Wireless Charging

Wireless chargers are convenient, but are they efficient?

Input: 20 Watts. Useful (into battery): 14 Watts.

1. Division (Useful √∑ Total) 14 √∑ 20 = 0.7
2. Percentage 0.7 √ó 100
3. Answer 70% Efficient

Where did the other 30% go? (Heat - that's why the phone gets warm).

Gaming Hardware

PS5 / Xbox Series X

These consoles use about 200 Watts of power.

If the fans are blowing out hot air, is that efficient?

Scenario:

  • Input: 200 J/s
  • Useful (Computing/Graphics): 120 J/s
  • Wasted (Heat): 80 J/s
Calculate Efficiency (120 √∑ 200) √ó 100 = 60%

Spot the Mistake

The Magic Motor

Scenario: A motor uses 100J of electrical energy to lift a box (150J of work).

Student Tom's Working:
Efficiency = (150 √∑ 100) √ó 100
Efficiency = 1.5 √ó 100
Efficiency = 150%
‚ùå The Error:

Impossible Physics! You cannot get out more energy than you put in.

Tom either measured wrong or he broke the laws of the universe.

Efficiency can NEVER be over 100%.

Module 4: Boss Battle

Harder Questions & Conservation of Energy

"Energy is not created or destroyed. It just changes form."

The Golden Rule

Conservation of Energy

Top GPE = Bottom KE

If a skater has 2000J of GPE at the top of a ramp...

They will have 2000J of KE at the bottom (ignoring friction).

Multi-Step Problem

The Half-Pipe

Skater Mass: 60kg. Ramp Height: 5m.

Step 1: Calculate GPE at top 60 √ó 10 √ó 5 = 3,000 J
Step 2: How much KE at bottom? 3,000 J (It converts!)
Step 3: What happens if they brake? Energy turns into Heat (Friction)

The Final Boss

Rearranging the Formula

Only for advanced students. Skip if class is struggling.

We know the GPE is 500J. The Mass is 5kg.

How High is the object?

GPE = m √ó g √ó h
1. Substitute 500 = 5 √ó 10 √ó h
2. Simplify 500 = 50 √ó h
3. Divide 500 √∑ 50 = 10 meters
