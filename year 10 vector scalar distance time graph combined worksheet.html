1. The Fundamentals (Easy)

SCALAR | VECTOR | DISTANCE | DISPLACEMENT | MAGNITUDE
a) A number with size but NO direction is a: SCALAR
b) A number with size AND direction is a: VECTOR
c) The full length of the actual path you walked is called: DISTANCE
d) The straight-line shortcut from start to finish is called: DISPLACEMENT
e) The scientific word for "size" or "amount" is: MAGNITUDE

Note: Because displacement is a vector, we can graph movement backwards past the start line using negative values.

1. Crossing the Origin (Medium)

a) What does the "0m" line represent on the Y-axis?
Ans: The starting position (the origin).
b) During "Stage 2", what is the object doing?
Ans: Standing still 10 meters away from the start.
c) At the "End" point, where is the object located compared to where it started?
Ans: It is 10 meters BEHIND its starting point (-10m).

2. Distance vs Displacement - The Same Journey Side-by-Side

A student walks 5m forwards, stops, then walks 10m backwards.

Graph A: Distance-Time
Graph B: Displacement-Time
Why does Graph A end at 15m, but Graph B ends at -5m? Explain the difference.
Ans: Distance (Graph A) totals all steps (5m + 10m = 15m). Displacement (Graph B) tracks position relative to start (5m forward, then 10m back lands you at -5m).

3. True or False Challenges

1. A distance graph line can NEVER slope downwards.
A) True B) False
Ans: A (True. Distance totals can only increase or stay the same).
2. A flat line below the X-axis on a displacement graph means you are moving backwards.
A) True B) False
Ans: B (False. A flat line ALWAYS means stopped. You are just stopped in a negative position).

3. Final Application Concepts

A GPS watch tracks a runner. The runner goes 5km East, then 5km West back to her house. The watch says she burned 500 calories. Does the calorie burn depend on her Distance or Displacement? Explain.
Ans: Distance. Her body expended energy for the entire 10km path walked. If it relied on displacement (0km), it would say she burned 0 calories!
If you drop a ball from a roof, it speeds up as it falls due to gravity. What would the Distance-Time graph of its fall look like?
Ans: A curve bending upwards (getting steeper), showing acceleration.
